Learn to speak the business language. Each biz/industry unique terminology/dialect - insert it into resume. If they can det which fit your skillset and align with professional goals. Hard skills = teachable and measurable abilities. Leanred ability thru edu and exp. Computer skills/forklift oper and phlebotomy. Get an idea of diff hard skills by diff industry. Variety of diff hard skills. Get an idea and incorporate right into resume.
Soft skills = make you a good employee. THink of as people skills. Comm, accountability, leadership. Incorp into resume in conjunction with each other.
Effective communicator with a hard skill, or strong leader with a hard skill.

Soft skills - unquantifiable attributes that CANNOT be proven. Subjective opinion on certain traits. Important to include in resume.
AI systems - scan resume by bots for required skills and qualifications. Can disqualify resume before it gets in front of recruiter. if not 10 of 15 skills listed it may be disqualified. 

How to ensure success in front of recruiter: reviewing job desc, tailor resume for the position. Keywords, qualifications, skills that align with the position. AI programs scan for keywords directly from the job desc. Most AI systems transfer pdf into text only version - word doc over PDF. 
Apply to position with a word doc and also a PDF. Two diff formats, locks in formatting. 
The formatting is locked in.

Keep the format simple. 
Spell out important keywords / terminology
Stick with simple bullets
Quantify results
Driving revenue, managing certain number of staff, cutting costs, try to put a number on something. 
Check resume in plaintext converter to break it down.

What to avoid on a resume:
putting important information in a header and footer. Put it in body of resume.
Avoid fancy format - info lost in conversion to plain text.
Stay away from complicated graphics, tables, columns (unless graphic design position). Avoid typos and poor grammar. Second eyes on resume.

A skills-based resume has name/contact/header on top. Summary of qualifications (overview of self as a professional). Include a bullet or two outside of pro work environment (ie volunteer). # of years experience in world and job applying to. Credentials, edu, training. Qualifications, key accomplishment, total qualification, important skills. 

Then summary of skills section bullet points - three column bullets.

Professional skills - 2-3 major skills on the one page that support your accomplishment.

Work history - business name, title, month/year. Edu, training, military and volunteer experience. Very flexible, creative. Sales and customer service revenue gen. Lay out your skills so recruiter can really see that and move into pro skills section.

### Part 2 Cover Letters
Important to write a new unique cover letter for each position apply to. Don't send the same for 6-7 different positions. Basic CL template, tailor to job applied to. Include hiring manager's name if available, Dear Jane Smith. Include specific examples of how you meet the qualifications listed in the job posting.
Guess if it meets those minimum qualifications. 
If looking for 5Y customer associates, you have 10Y bachelors, put it up front, no guessing / early disqualification.

CL should go in greater detail in resume. Explain situation like gaps of employment and other potential barriers. Clear concise and relate directly to the job applying for, CL should be 1 page.

Intro with what they're hiring, then your own qualifications for it. Then bulleted think higher level skills.

### Part 5 helpful tips
Be strategic when devving resume. List what skills and exp are relevant, leave out unnecessary info
Always tailor resume to job applying for. 
Keep your resume to 1-2 pages.
Quantify where applicable numbers for accomplishments.
Social media - include link to professional accounts on resume like Linkedin.
References no need unless requested, if needed list them on a separate page.
Include a professional email. Easy and fast to create one with services like Gmail.

Exp 10-15 yrs ago you can leave some experience off. They want current relevant skills.
Transparency - clear on resume do not lie about exp, skills, edu etc.
Achievements - highlight awards and achievements earned throughout career.
When interviewing for job, need to present self as best candidate for the job.
Have several docs ready to provide: cover letter, resume, references page, thank you email/letter. If any further questions feel free to reach out.
Hand out to each interview to access when interviewing.

DOL - various online databases to assit job search. Include NYS Job Bank and Virtual Career Center. Businesses can view them. Legitimate sites - never include any personally identifiable information. Individuals may be scammed/spammed diff info to identify. Just be careful when using other sites.

Question asked on skills based resume vs linkedin list of skills. Which are most high value, managing dilution, or some skills getting lost in the list:
A: For linkedin profile - include high level overview skills. For the resume, include skills directly related to the job. Some might be cross over. Esp applying in the same industry. Resume tailor to individual job desc. Linkedin is more high level 5-8 what your skills are and some bullets supporting those.

Transferable skills makes sense if looking at what new career is looking for. Look at what positions that are close or not. Medical, individual law field etc. Customer service, meet in the middle and be important etc. Focus on transferable skills, incorporate right in resume.

If not meeting skills/qualifications, they will train you up.
