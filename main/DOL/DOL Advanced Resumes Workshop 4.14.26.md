Accomplishment statements are not just job duties. Accomplished [x] as measured by [y] by completing [z]
Do not just list duties / what you were supposed to do. To impress potential employer, showcase accomplishment = how you contributed to org goals, show initiative, went above and beyond, added value, positive impact.

Planned events -> organized a sold-out charity event for 300 people and raised $125,000.
Sent marketing emails about products/services -> designed 100+ email campaigns averaging a Click-Through Rate (CTR) of 3% and driving over $2M in sales.
Led training sessions -> conducted compliance training for 80+ managers across three locations.

Craft powerful bullet points: Focus on achievements, quantify your success, use the STAR method.
Managed, developed, implemented, created, led, resolved, collaborated, etc. These are more impactful than "responsible for" or "duties included" or "assisted with" (doesn't take enough ownership of work).

When stuck writing bullets: some things you can do. Developing accomplishment statements can be tricky. Brainstorm more info.
How have things changed since you began the job and what contributions did you make to impact that change?
Did you assume any responsibilities outside of your job description, how can you highlight that commitment to your role?
What would have happened if you had not done your job?
What sets you apart from others with similar skills and experience? (differentiator)
What was the need for dept or company when you were hired?

Avoid redundancies. Repeating bullets, words, stating the obvious, generic phrases, and overlapping information. These waste space / dilute msg / seem less efficient.
