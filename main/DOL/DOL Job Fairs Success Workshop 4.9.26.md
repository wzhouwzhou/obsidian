Recruiter or representative at the table

Why attend job fairs:
While may not walk away with job offer, walk away with valuable info and connections.
1. Opportunity to learn more about companies hiring. Gives idea of which businesses looking to hire and allows to learn more about them.
2. Help focus on companies actively seeking employees. Target search more to companies with openings = determine careerr direction. Biz in your area and positions. Can help guide you which jobs to target/
3. Market yourself, talk to employers, make connections with employers other organizations or other job seekers. Can make valuable connections who can provide helpful information. HR professionals may be sometimes difficult to reach. Not impersonal online. Chance to speak to rep from a company. Impression with eye contact and enthusiasm, face to name, resume and CL alone may not be able to do this. 

Finding upcoming job fairs:
- DOL events calendar, and filter by region. statistics.labor.ny.gov/career-zone/career-calendar.shtml
- Local career centers dol.ny.gov/career-centers
- Company websites - company social media pages
- Newspaper
- Chamber of Commerce

Register ahead of time = puts name on attendees list, to employers and organizers. Sometimes can upload resume ahead of time.

Research = list of companies registered to be at the career fair. Online, see what openings they have, go to career page on website and see if any openings that would be a good opening. Find a reason want to work for company - products, mission statement, heard from employees great place to work etc. Research company size, current info, news (new product, move offices etc). 

Research resources = use google search on company, see what comes up. Might be useful to review annual reports, sales catalogues, brochures. Customers/clients, someone who works there, etc. Google/People/Company websites/LinkedIn/Glassdoor.

Create a plan or strategy like which employers you want to talk to first - review list of companies attending that you want to talk to. Visit every table at fair if time permits. Find most important ones to you so you can talk to them.

Get resume ready electronically, and plenty of paper copies. Some do not accept hard copy = may need to post on a website or complete online app. Be ready to discuss qualifications. Org skills and confidence. Mini resume on business card potential - contact info on one side, skills list on another. Copy on phone to ready to email immediately. Email yourself resume, then forward from email app on phone. Customised resume for each role. 

Elevator pitch = includes yourself, bg, key skills, Why you're interested in working on the company, align with company motto.
Hello {person}, Joe made me promise toi say hello to you today. We had been talking about changing xyz in the industry. I have [degree] and [5Y experience].

Prepare to answer questions like "What are you here for today", Ask: "What do you need to do to obtain an interview, what are common career paths in org, what do you look for in a candidate, what advice do you have for applicants, how long have you been working there and what do you like about working there?"
NYSDOL InterviewStream tool = you can record yourself and add back dol.ny.gov/interviewstream

During the fair:
Arrive early. You're dedicated and will be prompt once you get the job.
Dress professionally. First impression. Leave coat and extra items in the car if possible.
Silence cell phone. Don't get caught disrespectful in interview or discussion with recruiter.
Bring a pen, note pad, folder with resumes.

Every interaction is mini interview / mini network contact.

Quick review of the room. Any companies new getting to event, core companies first, even if want to go to all.

Talk to people = firm handshake, eye contact, smile, ask relevant questions. Bring hand sanitizer. Name + 30s commercial. Prepared knowledge, answer questions you prepared to demo knowledge of field. Your chance to hear from their POV.

Tips for success:
- Obtain contact info
- Find out the next steps. When and how best to follow up, email or phone?
- Thank them for their time. 

Talk with other job seekers at job fair. Professional, polite, positive, mindful of employer time. If they seem busy with other priorities don't talk too long. Advantage of other services may be onsite interview, resume review / distribution, computers for online apps, etc.

After the job fair, get organized, send thank you emails, follow up.
Use a job fair log to take notes after the job fair. Helps you track who you spoke with and what followup steps you need to do. 

No casual, food/drinks/gum, bring anyone not looking for a job, no friends and family (unprofessional) - available but not speaking for you, taking too much employer's time, venting about past employers, and don't ask about salary and benefits.

Visit every table = keep an open mind. Bring plenty of resumes, act as if going to interview, good first impression, may be interviewed on the spot, don't expect a job offer, make valuable contacts, have fun.

Virtual job fairs = connect with busineses live = may have preview day and then live day. Connect during live day, on events calendar. Virtual fair training are each week + on events calendar.
