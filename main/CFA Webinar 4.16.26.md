The methodology of how we connect to the survey. To better understand future clientelle,

2400 investors: surveyed across 6 major markets representing 45%+ global personal wealth assets. Canada, India, SG, UAE, UK, and US. Across three wealth tiers. Within those markets, survey in particular at least mass affluent, HNW, or very high net worth. 
Look into different generations are behaving or thinking. Focus on gen Z. Ages 18-28. And millenial 29 to 44 age. Identify key prefs and behavs of those cohorts and then rel gen x and baby boomers.
A lot of diff ways to slice up the data. Most meaningful patterns emerging from generational lines. The key of report focusing on diffs between generations. Highlight wealth level and market level distinctions where affecting findings.

Next Gen Investors report structure = 4 main sections. What are young people looking for. Portfolio posture. How investors make decisions. And finally how advisors can help with practical solution for pitfalls (ie FOMO).

Advisor role goes from expert to a partner in rpactice.

Ryan Munson: the good news is that the desire for formal financial advice is strong and continuing to grow. 90%+ gen Z and millenials use some form of fin advice. However, how they are using financial advice is changing. They don't simply want to delegate decisions to advisors. Want to be collaborators, co-create fin strategy. Learn and participate in execution of goal seeking.
Advisory relationship as more collab partnership.
Trust and ethics remain top trait across all generations for what makes them willing to and comfortable with working with an advisor. 
As we look specifically gen Z and millennials. Trust = defined thru functional and measurable criterial. Data security, pro credentials, transparency, and conflicts of interest.
Advisor goal is evolving from gatekeeper of info (needed advisor to execute a trade) to more of a curator or translator of information in the onslaught of info available digitally. 
In function = more of a coaching model / mentorship where an investor comes with ideas or thoughts about how something might fit into strategy to help achieve goals. Advisor overlays their exp, knowledge, wisdom to try figure if that's the right solution, here's why, why not, help them understand implications of decisions that they're making.

Ryan: mentioning ai. The technology lens is applied in a couple of ways. Robo/digital advisory competitors to trad fin advisors initially but millennials and only some gen Z, it's more a compliment than a competitor.
