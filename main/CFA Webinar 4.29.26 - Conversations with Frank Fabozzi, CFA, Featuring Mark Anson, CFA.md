Q: Where do you see genuine opportunity vs potential overheating, and sust themes vs cyclical trends.
Priv Credit is not cyclical trend. Largest parts of private capital space.; It was seen as shadow banking initially. Today diff. Mainstream asset class. Allocating to it at comonfund as part of SAA for about 8 yrs. 
Not cyclical and sust because large/small banks pull from trad biz model from making/servicing loan to a corp, take it, put it on BS and service, collect int, and how to gen profits. Biz model for banking sector changing from loan orig/managing the loans to fee gen for other services. Now off wealth mgmt, custody fees, fedex trading and clearing. Capturing fees than collecting rent from portfolio. That trend isn't going away so not cyclical. Banks pulling away from trad route create a void. Esp to SMEs. 
Private credit flourishes there. 
One of few places where arbitrage opportunity. Precise = riskless profits. More generally = get more than you should. That's the case with priv credit. Focus on senior secured direct lending to SMEs. 
Looking at priv credit and SS loans of similar quality to HY bonds. Senior secured loans earn more. And that should not be the case. Start with fact senior secured top of cap structure. Earn more of HY bonds of similar quality at bottom of strucure.
Bonds have dur risk; senior secured lending at top do not have risk, adjusting floating rate risk. Senior secured floating rate debt should earn less than HY bond with lots of duration, but they earn more. Thus arbitrage.
Hence bullish on priv credit. See arb and while continues to exist, take advantage of it.

Look at things like litigation finance (financing claims in court). Aircraft leasing is another, leasing is another. Priv credit lots of places.

Q: Crises dotcom to GFC to pandemic, times of inflation and IR shocks. Exp and lessons of risk mgmt stayed constant across the diff envs.
A: Pay attn to econ fundamentals. Ie eqt risk premium. Return prem get for investing in public equities over US treasury bonds. Equities fundamentally more risky than tsy bonds. Should earn over long time some risk prem for owning public stock over tsy bonds. 
Look back 40-50 yrs, LT equity risk prem is about 4.1-4.2%. What you should earn public equity over tsy bond.
A long time ago, dotcom height CalPERS. What you make of stock mkt valuation. Answer at the time was unclear, Some analysis how we felt about it - put together equity risk prem calc. It's just an IRR or discount rate calc.
Forecast out earnings to SP500 to next 2/3/4 years. Figure disc rate to discount future earnings of SP500 to today's current value in today mkt. This is an IRR. Subtract the yield on the 10Y treasury bond = ERP. LT avg ~4.2%. At dot com, that equity risk prem was 0.
Fin mkts believed public stocks no more risky than bonds. You didn't even have risk prem to own them.
When plotting eqt risk prem back then 30-40Y. One other time at 0. 1987, other time, October 87 big crash - Black Monday Black October, when circuit breakers introduced at NYSE.
Back then portfolio insurance - you could elim equity risk thru port insurance. But you couldn't. But people believed you could, eqt prem 0. Dramatic correction. Risk prem shot back up.
Dot com, went to 0, burst, 3Y bear mkt in US equities.
2000-2002 double digit declines in US equity markets.
Board allowed underweight equities. Against the grain then. Credit CalPERS courage/fortitude to say overvalue and cut back alloc until sort them out. Equity fundamentals.

Continue thru career - commonfund macro dashboard. liq prem is one of the data points. ERP on quarterly basis too. Now, ERP is about 2.3%, LT avg 4.2%; stock overvalued vs LT avg.

Then extract testable hypotheses to build into computer program. Then construct the program, empirical paradigm based on predicted factors to test the hypotheses using econometric techniques. Review the data and draw conclusions of econometric data analysis on hypotheses. Conclude did your factors influence or not stock price behavior. This is a predictive model.
